Unit 8: Data Compliance

This unit examined data protection regulations, particularly GDPR, and their implications for data management practices. We explored compliance requirements, lawful bases for processing, and implementation strategies.

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand GDPR principles and requirements
  • Identify lawful bases for data processing
  • Apply compliance measures in data projects
  • Evaluate trade-offs between compliance and functionality

Unit Reflection

Criteria Experience Learning
What?
(Description)		 In this unit, I studied GDPR principles and participated in a collaborative discussion about compliance challenges in big data contexts. I analyzed how data protection requirements affect analytical capabilities and contributed my perspective to peer discussions. The unit covered the seven GDPR principles, data subject rights, and organisational obligations. Understanding the distinction between controllers and processors clarified responsibility allocation in data supply chains.
So what?
(Analysis)		 The collaborative discussion revealed diverse perspectives on balancing compliance with analytical utility. Engaging with peers' viewpoints challenged my initial assumptions about what "compliance" practically means for different types of organisations. I gained appreciation for why GDPR compliance is not simply a checklist exercise. The principles require ongoing attention and judgment calls, particularly around purpose limitation and data minimisation in big data contexts where potential uses evolve.
Now what?
(Outcome)		 I will integrate compliance considerations into early project planning rather than treating them as constraints to work around. Building privacy-by-design into data architectures will become standard practice. Professionally, given my role working with government data, I plan to pursue formal data protection training. Understanding compliance deeply is essential for responsible digital innovation in the public sector.
